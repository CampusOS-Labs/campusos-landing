## The paradox

India produces some of the world's sharpest minds. Yet walk into most of our 1.5M+ schools and you'll find fees tracked in Excel sheets, parent communication buried in WhatsApp groups, timetables that collapse when one teacher is absent, and report cards rushed with copy-pasted comments.

The talent density is incredible. The operational infrastructure hasn't caught up.

## The problem isn't adoption. It's architecture.

Schools don't resist better tools. They resist tools that don't fit. The market's answer has always been the monolithic ERP — a 200-feature platform that promises everything and delivers nothing useful. Schools buy it, fight it for six months, then default back to spreadsheets.

You cannot build one platform that works for 1.5M schools. Each has its own way of working, its own constraints, its own combination of broken workflows.

## Build what's broken, not what's exhaustive

Most software companies show up with a product and ask you to change how you work. We don't. We show up and ask: what's actually broken? Fees taking too long to reconcile? Parents missing notices? Timetables falling apart every month?

Tell us the one thing that hurts the most. Then we build just that. Not a 200-feature platform you'll never use. Not a system that requires you to change everything overnight. Just the piece of broken infrastructure you operate with every day — fixed, installed at your school, your staff trained on it.

## One workflow at a time

We're starting there because we believe:

- **Schools are not businesses.** Yet most software treats them like enterprises. We build for educators, not ERP consultants.
- **Spreadsheets are not a strategy.** Duct-taped tools create busywork, not insight. Schools deserve a cohesive operating system.
- **Open by default.** No black boxes, no vendor lock-in, no surprises.
- **India's talent deserves better.** The youth of this country wants to take a stand for a better education system. They deserve infrastructure that doesn't hold them back.

We're starting with schools, one workflow at a time.
