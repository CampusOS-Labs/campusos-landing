## What does it mean?
When I say a "school brain," I mean a system that can capture knowledge, store it reliably, and surface the right information instantly when someone needs it.

So what does that look like for a school or organization?

It means every important piece of information—from policies and processes to student records and institutional know-how—should be stored securely in one trusted place and be searchable in seconds.

## Introducing Slate

Slate is an AI agent that lives in your text messages.

Not another dashboard you have to log into. Not another app on your home screen collecting dust. Slate meets you where you already are — on WhatsApp, iMessage, or SMS — and turns a simple text into access to everything your school knows.

### Everything your school already has, connected

The school brain only works if it has something to remember. Slate indexes every piece of information your school already collects and uses:

- **Policies and processes** — admission procedures, leave policies, fee structures, safety protocols
- **Student records** — enrollment details, attendance history, academic progress, parent contacts
- **Institutional know-how** — the things that live in people's heads: which vendor to call, how field trips get approved, what to do when the AC breaks
- **Schedules and events** — teacher timetables, planned activities, calendar conflicts
- **Announcements** — what was sent, to whom, and when
- **Fee and billing** — payment status for every student, dues, receipts, reminders

All of this stays updated in real time. When a parent pays a fee, Slate knows. When an announcement goes out, Slate knows. When tomorrow's schedule changes, Slate knows.

### Ask anything. Get grounded answers.

You just text Slate about whatever you need to know or want it to do.

"What was last year's leave policy for teachers?"
"Has Riya Sharma's fee been paid this quarter?"
"What announcements did we send to Nursery parents this month?"
"Who is covering Mrs. Patel's class on Thursday?"

Slate doesn't guess. It retrieves actual information from your school's data — documents, records, policies, live operational context — and answers with specifics you can trust.

### Do things, not just look things up

Retrieval is only half of it. After Slate has the grounded facts, it can act on your behalf:

- Schedule meetings with parents or staff
- Draft and send replies to emails
- Push out announcements to the right WhatsApp groups
- Follow up on unpaid fees
- Coordinate around schedule changes

The sequence matters: **retrieve first, then act**. Slate looks up what it needs to know before doing anything. No made-up answers. No sending the wrong message to the wrong parents. No scheduling a meeting when someone's already booked.

### The interface is a text message

That's the point. Your school already runs on WhatsApp groups, phone calls, and quick messages between staff. Slate doesn't ask you to change that. It asks you to text it like you'd text a colleague who happens to know everything about your school — and who can actually get things done.
